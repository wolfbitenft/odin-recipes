My Favourite Garlic Bread Recipe

Toasted Garlic Bread

This recipe is brought to you by SimplyRecipes.

Garlic bread is one of my favourite side dishes. It's so simple, easy to make, and yummy!

If you're interested in keeping vampires at bay, keep reading.

Pro tip: Avoid this recipe if you're trying to bag Lestat de Lioncourt.

Ingredients

  • 1 16-ounce loaf Italian bread or French bread
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 large cloves garlic, smashed and minced
  • 1 heaping tablespoon chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese, optional

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
  2. Cut the loaf in half, horizontally. Mix the butter, garlic, and parsley together in a small bowl. Spread butter mixture over the two bread halves.
  3. Place the halves of bread on a sturdy baking pan and heat in an oven for 10 minutes.
  4. Remove the pan from the oven. Sprinkle optional Parmesan cheese over bread. Return to oven on the highest rack.
  5. Broil on high heat for 2-3 minutes until the edges of the bread begin to toast (and the cheese bubbles). Watch closely as bread can burn easily.
  6. Remove from oven and let cool for a minute. Remove from pan and use a bread knife to cut into 1-inch thick slices. Serve immediately.

Soft Garlic Bread

If you prefer your garlic bread soft instead of toasted and crunchy, this is the method for you.

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
  2. Mix the butter, garlic, and parsley together in a small bowl.
  3. Make 1-inch thick slices into the bread, but don't go all the way through -- just to the bottom crust. Put a teaspoon or two of the butter mixture between each slice, or between every other slice if you only want the garlic mixture on one side of the bread.
  4. Wrap the bread in aluminum foil and heat for 15 minutes in the oven, until the butter has melted and the bread has warmed.
  5. To serve, remove from foil and present the loaf wrapped in a clean tea towel. Tear off slices to eat.
